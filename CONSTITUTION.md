# The Human-Core Constitution

These are the non-negotiable principles of the Human-Core Initiative. They are the test every contribution must pass. They cannot be overridden by contribution, funding, partnership, or convenience. A change that conflicts with anything here is out of scope, however good it looks.

This document is lifted from the founding proposal. The founding proposal remains the fuller statement of why.

---

## The principles

Each is a short rule you can hold in your head and use to judge a decision.

**Build the human before the skill.** Development rests on sleep, movement, play, language, and a present adult who talks and listens. These are the foundation, not the extras, and they come first.

**The child does the thinking.** Learning is the residue of the child's own mental effort. Any tool, screen, adult, or AI that does the thinking for the child is removed, however impressive.

**Knowledge before skill.** Critical thinking, creativity, and judgment grow from a deep base of knowledge. Build the base, and the higher abilities become possible.

**Meet the child where they are.** Teach at the child's actual level, not the level of the curriculum and not the top of the class. Move them forward from there.

**Hard but achievable.** Guide the child's effort at the edge of what they can do. Effortful success is where learning and curiosity live; boredom and defeat are where they die.

**Take what works, from anywhere.** We build on the strongest global evidence about how humans actually learn, and we drop what is merely fashionable or familiar, however beloved.

## The two commitments

Alongside the principles, two commitments hold.

**Free and open, forever.** Curriculum and documentation are free, forkable, and translatable, owned by no company and adoptable by anyone on earth. Derivatives stay open.

**The boundary.** Our tools serve the human. We never build systems that infer the emotional or inner states of children. This is stated in full in the next section.

---

## The boundary, stated once and globally

The initiative draws one hard line, and it holds everywhere, not only under European law.

**We do not build systems that infer the emotional or inner states of children.**

The reason is not merely regulatory. It is that such inference is scientifically unreliable, context-dependent, and aimed at people who cannot refuse it. Everything the original vision wanted from it, understanding the struggling child and helping the adult respond, is delivered better by pointing the technology at the teaching and at development. We lose nothing real and gain a foundation that survives scrutiny anywhere.

This is a feature to celebrate, never to soften.

---

## What the teacher tool must never do

Pillar 2 puts an AI coach in the hands of teachers. By constitution, that tool must never:

- infer a child's emotion or inner state;
- score, rank, or profile children;
- replace the teacher's relationship with the child.

One critical design constraint follows: the tool is owned by the teacher and private to the teacher. The moment it becomes a dashboard for administrators to monitor staff, it stops being coaching and becomes surveillance of teachers. Its data belongs to the person being developed, not to the institution.

---

## The guardrails for children's AI work

Pillar 3 teaches children to use, question, and out-think AI. These guardrails are non-negotiable in every session and in any fork of the material:

- Children never type personal information, their own or anyone else's, into an AI tool.
- No AI is ever used to analyze, score, or guess the feelings or states of the children.
- No AI is used to make content about real, identifiable people without consent, and never about other children.
- The teacher operates or supervises all AI use.
- Children are encouraged to question AI, including the school's own AI tools. That questioning is the lesson.

## Governance

- **License.** Curriculum and documentation under Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike. Software under a permissive open-source license. Free forever, derivatives kept open. See [LICENSE](LICENSE) and [NOTICE](NOTICE).
- **Constitution.** The principles above, including the boundary, cannot be overridden by contribution.
- **Evidence standard.** Claims cite their evidence. Critique that strengthens the base is a valued contribution.
- **Multilingual from the start.** Founding work in English for reach, German and Italian as first translations, open invitation to localize anywhere.
- **Research partnership.** A research institution validates and stress-tests the pedagogy, so it is more than one practitioner's conviction.

We will publish what fails as openly as what works. A framework that hides its null results is not evidence-based.
