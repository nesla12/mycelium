# The Human-Core AI Curriculum
## A modular programme for schools, 6 to 24 hours

### Drop-in AI mastery for informatics, science, mathematics, the humanities, or standalone. Ages 10 to 14. Free, open, no signup.

This is the implementation layer of the Human-Core Initiative. The lessons themselves live in three companion documents: the Pillar 3 Teacher Workbook (foundational, ages 10 to 12), Pillar 3 Part II The Frontier (advanced, ages 12 to 14), and the Facilitator's Guide. This document is how a teacher or a director assembles those lessons into a programme that fits the hours they have and the subject they teach, and multiplies their students' real AI skills quickly.

You do not adopt a philosophy. You pick a track, slot it into a subject you already teach, and run it. Everything is ready to use, including for a teacher who is not an AI expert and a school with no budget.

---

## How to choose, in two questions

1. **How many hours can you give it?** Pick a pathway: 6, 12, 18, or 24 hours.
2. **Where does it live?** Inside informatics, science, mathematics, or the humanities, or as a standalone unit. The subject maps below show which modules fit where.

That is the whole decision. The rest of this document supports it.

## Pre-built pathways

### 6 hours: AI Essentials
**Modules:** F1, F2, F3, F4, F8.
**By the end:** every student can use AI without being fooled by it. They understand what it is, they try their own thinking first, they catch its fabrications, they verify what they do not know, and they have reasoned about honest use.
**For:** any school that wants the single most important AI literacy in the shortest time. Fits a short informatics or science unit.

### 12 hours: AI Core
**Modules:** all eight foundational, F1 through F8.
**By the end:** full foundational AI literacy. Students use, question, verify, prompt, edit, and ethically reason about AI, and can defend their own work.
**For:** ages 10 to 12, a half-term unit or a term of weekly lessons.

### 18 hours: AI Mastery
**Modules:** all eight foundational, plus A1, A4, A5, A8.
**By the end:** foundational literacy plus the frontier essentials, how reasoning models work, the modern research workflow, deliberate cognitive self-control, and reasoning about their own future.
**For:** stronger or older groups, ages 11 to 13, a full term.

### 24 hours: The Full Programme
**Modules:** all sixteen, run with the Facilitator's Guide for the debate-heavy units, ending in the capstone project.
**By the end:** students can use, audit, and out-think AI, reason about ownership, power, collaboration, and the future, and have started closing gaps in their own education.
**For:** a full course, ages 10 to 14, ideally spiralled across two years with foundational first.

## Subject integration maps

The programme is built to live inside subjects you already teach. Here is the hook for each.

**Informatics and computing (the natural home).** F1, F5, F6, A1, A2. Students learn what these systems actually are, how to direct them, how bias enters, how they reason, and what they cost. This turns a computing class from teaching tools that will be obsolete into teaching judgment that will not.

**Science.** F3, F4, A4. The scientific method is verification, sourcing, and skepticism, which is exactly what these modules train. AI hallucination becomes a live lesson in evidence, and the modern research workflow strengthens every investigation students do afterward.

**Mathematics.** A1, F4, A2. Reasoning models make the evaluation of a chain of logic concrete. Evaluating AI statistics and claims is applied numeracy. Compute and scaling bring real numbers about energy and cost.

**Humanities, language, and ethics.** F7, F8, A3, A6. Authorship, honesty, voice, ownership, and collaboration are humanities questions at their core, and AI makes them urgent and real rather than abstract.

**Standalone, life skills, or careers.** F2, A5, A6, A7, A8. If there is no obvious host subject, these modules stand alone as a unit on thinking well, working with others, and steering your own life in a changing world.

## Scheduling

- **Weekly.** One module per week. The 12-hour Core fits a term, the 24-hour Full fits a year.
- **Block or intensive.** A project week, an activity day, or a holiday programme. Run a pathway in consecutive sessions.
- **Cross-curricular.** Split the modules across subjects by the maps above, so informatics, science, and the humanities each carry a few. This is the lightest load on any single teacher.

## For the director: the one-page case

**Why adopt this.** Your students will live and work inside AI. Most schools are either banning it or letting it quietly erode students' thinking. This programme does neither. It makes students its masters: able to use it, catch its errors, and stay in charge of their own minds. That is the most future-proof thing you can teach right now.

**What it costs you.** Between 6 and 24 hours of existing curriculum time, no budget, and no requirement that the teacher be an AI expert. The materials are complete and include everything needed to run offline if necessary.

**What students gain.** Concrete, demonstrable skills: explaining how AI works, catching fabrications, verifying claims, prompting well, editing and owning their work, reasoning about ethics, and, in the advanced track, evaluating reasoning, researching properly, and thinking about power and their own future.

**Standards.** The programme maps to the four dimensions of the UNESCO AI Competency Framework for Students (2024): human-centred mindset, ethics of AI, AI techniques and applications, and critical judgment. You can tell parents and authorities, truthfully, that you teach AI to a recognized international standard.

**What it is not.** It is not screen time, not a coding bootcamp, and not a tool that surveils or scores your students. By constitution, nothing in it senses or infers children's emotions or inner states.

**What you need to start.** One willing teacher, the three companion documents, and a pathway chosen from this guide. That is all.

## Knowing it works

Keep the evidence light and honest.
- **The can-do checklist** in the Teacher Workbook lists the concrete skills. Tick them as students demonstrate them.
- **The markup assessment** in the Workbook gives every student one AI answer to mark for a fabrication, a bias, and an unsupported claim. It is the single best end-of-programme check.
- **Simple pre and post.** Before and after, ask students to spot what is wrong in a flawed AI answer. The difference is your result.
- Share what does not work as openly as what does. That honesty is what makes this programme trustworthy and what improves it.
